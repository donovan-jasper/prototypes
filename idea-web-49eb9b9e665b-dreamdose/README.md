# RestPulse

A mobile app that guides users into optimal short rest or power nap states, actively preventing deep sleep to ensure they wake refreshed and alert without grogginess.

**Who it's for:** Students needing quick mental resets between study sessions, professionals optimizing work breaks for increased focus, commuters wanting to rest without missing their stop, individuals seeking to manage fatigue without full sleep, and anyone interested in biohacking or enhancing their productivity through optimized short rests.

**Gap:** Existing solutions either focus on helping users fall into deep sleep (meditation/sleep apps) or passively track sleep. There's a significant gap for an app that actively and intelligently intervenes with subtle cues (audio, haptic, light) to *prevent* deep sleep during a controlled rest period, ensuring users wake up refreshed and avoid grogginess. Most 'power nap' apps are just timers, lacking the 'intelligent' and 'preventative' aspects.

**Monetization:** Freemium model with a subscription for premium features. The free tier could offer basic power nap timers and a limited selection of relaxation sounds. A premium subscription ($5-10/month or $50-80/year) would unlock advanced features like: personalized adaptive cue systems, a wider library of curated soundscapes and haptic patterns, smart home integration (e.g., Philips Hue), detailed rest analytics, and exclusive guided 'light rest' sessions. People would pay for the tangible benefits of increased alertness, improved productivity, and the unique ability to control their rest state without oversleeping.

**Viability: 7/10 | Competition: 7/10 | Difficulty: Medium — While basic timers and soundscapes are easy, implementing the 'intelligent' aspect to subtly prevent deep sleep requires sophisticated algorithms for cue timing and intensity, potentially leveraging user feedback or even basic sensor data (though accurate sleep stage detection without medical-grade sensors is challenging). Integrating with smart home devices adds complexity. The core challenge is delivering on the promise of 'intelligent' and 'preventative' without over-promising what a phone can achieve.**
